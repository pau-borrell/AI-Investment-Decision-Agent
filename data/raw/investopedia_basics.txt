Diversification is a risk management strategy that mixes a wide variety of investments within a portfolio. The rationale behind this technique is that a portfolio constructed of different kinds of assets will, on average, yield higher long-term returns and lower the risk of any individual holding.

Stocks represent ownership in a company. Investors buy stocks to gain from capital appreciation and dividends. Stocks can be volatile, especially in the short term.

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) are investment funds that are traded on stock exchanges. ETFs hold assets such as stocks, bonds, or commodities and generally operate with lower fees than mutual funds.

The S&P 500 is a stock market index that tracks 500 of the largest companies in the United States. It is commonly used as a benchmark for the overall market.

Risk tolerance refers to the degree of variability in investment returns that an investor is willing to withstand.
