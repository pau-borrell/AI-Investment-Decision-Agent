ETFs are commonly used to achieve diversification because they provide exposure to a broad range of assets in a single investment.

For example, an S&P 500 ETF provides exposure to large-cap U.S. equities across multiple sectors.

Diversification reduces unsystematic risk, which is the risk associated with individual assets.

However, diversification does not eliminate systematic risk, which affects the entire market.

Investors should consider sector exposure when building a portfolio to avoid over-concentration in a single industry.
